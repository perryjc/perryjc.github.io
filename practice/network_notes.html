Oct. 15 vocab

  1. Protocol:
      How devices communicate over the internet with each other.
      Internet Protocol (or IP) is the address of a device (ex: 54.87.133.155 is the IP of the instructure servers)
  2. Packet:
      Little bits of info sent over a network, all packets are the same size.
      most packets are pieces of a bigger "puzzle"
  3. Header:
      The basic info of a protocol, stuff like To:IP and From:IP and how many Packets the data being sent is made up of.
  4. Payload:
      What each packet is carrying, this could be things like text
  5. Router:
      Handles the actual movement of HTTP protocols sending them to diffrent routers till the target IP is connected
